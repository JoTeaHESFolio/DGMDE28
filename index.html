Introduction

From an early age, my fascination with farming was kindled by my father's deep-rooted connection to agriculture. Raised among chickens in Connecticut, he carried this affinity with him to Florida. He embarked on a journey of cultivating what he endearingly termed a 'Gentleman's Farm,' first in Florida and then in Northeastern Pennsylvania.

During my pre-teen years, I eagerly embraced this heritage, learning to operate and maintain tractors as I carefully navigated the upper fields, avoiding the tender tree saplings. Alongside my mother, I honed my skills in tending to our one-acre food production field—gardening, harvesting, and preserving our bountiful produce. I even raised prize-winning pheasants, unwittingly contributing plump, wholesome fowl to holiday tables across seveal neighboring farms during Thanksgiving and Christmas.

I voraciously delved into books on herbs and foraging, worked at rejuvenating acres of wildflowers and several long-forgotten heritage rose bushes and peonies neglected by previous gardeners.

My family would return to Florida each fall, where I attended high school. I took up Latin to enrich my botanical vocabulary, participated in science fairs, and delved into the arts. My father's guidance in developing science fair projects led me down a path that embraced alternate energy sources, unknowingly sowing the seeds of environmentalism. By the time I graduated, I was well-versed in solar and hydrogen energy systems. And then like more teens who grow up on farms, I left it all behind to pursue a different career.

For the next 25 years, I pursued studies and practiced in the arts and humanities while maintaining a simple kitchen garden. It wasn't until the aftermath of Hurricane Matthew devastated my community that I returned to farming with renewed purpose.

As I witnessed the vulnerabilities in our food distribution systems during the pandemic, my commitment to sustainable practices deepened. This led me to return to college where I explored subjects ranging from religion and war to sustainable practices theory and nutrition. I went "all-in" despite being fully virtual — participating in hack-a-thons with the US Census Department and developing a business in the Harvard i-lab during my undergraduate studies.

Between academic terms, I dedicated myself to continuous learning, enrolling in MOOCs and certificate programs focused on climate change and regenerative practices. I even began pursuing an MS didactic nutrition program with the goal of becoming a Registered Dietary Nutritionist (RDN).

Today, I am finishing my graduate degree at Harvard in Digital Media Design and serving as an Americorps VISTA with Forsyth Farmers' Market. I utilize the knowledge attained in my studies, and the practical experience from my earlier career, to contribute to addressing food equity and security issues in my community. One of my weekly responsibilities is maintaining the organization website—the Forsyth Farmer's Market, hosted by Square Space. In the next five years, I aspire to pursue a Ph.D. While Harvard University is my top choice, I am also exploring opportunities at Tufts' University in their Agriculture, Food, and Environment program.

Ultimately, my vision extends beyond Earth's borders. I aspire to innovate in food systems redesign and develop sustainable food cultivation practices for off-planet exploration—but not for any of Elon Musk's ventures. In finding creative ways to address the challenges of nourishing humans for long-haul travel beyond our planet, we can find solutions that drive meaningful changes in food systems design and distribution here on Earth. Food, after all, is a fundamental human right, and I am dedicated to being an agent of change, working towards equity—or ensuring healthy, nutrient-dense, whole foods for EVERYONE—while safeguarding our planet.

Background in Web and Software Development

I grew up with a father who coded—and was legally blind.

Yes, the same father who farmed—that was his hobby. His profession was mechanical engineering—he was a 'brilliant mind' type, without a college degree. He dabbled in everything. So, he showed me basic C, C++, COBOL, and Fortran... shockingly, I remember none of it! Sure, I can look at a sample of code and fairly well identify it. However, I am fairly well the CompSci equivalent of a 'Beauty School Drop Out!'

My ADHD and compounded dyslexic issues got the better of me in school... and still presents a challenge, particularly with scripting language. I manage.

Below is a list of proficiencies. I am most comfortable with Python—but could not tell you why. And I am literate and functional in all listed languages, though not really practiced in all of them (except JS—I just suck at JS!). I am familiar with React, but I do not yet feel comfortable or competent enough to list it on my resume.

Languages - in order of preference

  • Python
  • HTML
  • CSS3
  • R
  • Scratch
  • SQL
  • JS or JavaScript

I have some exposure to Wolfram, and I am working on teaching myself through their website. I also have experience with JSON. Two classes I took with Professor Mikhak provided exposure to React, but I did not work with it for my final project, opting for the 'no code' work product.

As to my web development experience, I have some basic projects completed for classes in Scratch and HTML. My JS project with an API call for my last class was a flop. My preference is to work with no-code options—I prefer WIX and Notion; I also like what Canva is doing. I have also worked in Framer, and as I mentioned in my bio, I work with Square Space too, but I am not wild about it. One of my points of pride—and that which keeps me interested in coding— was work on a team project in an AI and Robotics course that created a program to analyze the condition (specifically, the colors in a fruit) to determine its level of 'ripeness.'

Overall, I am not confident as a 'coder.' I understand it takes practice. And while I will continue to work at building my skills, I know that I will be better at assisting others in assessing code and developing a project. I am a 'big picture' person, and I excel at scoping, planning out the work, and ensuring that jobs are completed on time and on budget.

I chose to step into this course to help improve my skills and confidence with both coding and web design, and especially coding in JS and React.
